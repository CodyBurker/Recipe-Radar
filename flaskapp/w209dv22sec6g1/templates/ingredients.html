Given the number of ingredients that humans eat, the total number of ways to combine them is on the order of fifteen exponential significant figures. Yet the actual number of recipes we eat is very small, so an ongoing challenge for food scientists is to discover laws that govern flavor combinations and use them to create new recipes yet to be experienced by human taste buds.
